# Screen To Video Bridge Examples

These examples are synthetic bridge references.
They are not screenplay replacements.

## 1. Stronger Bridge

### Source Scene Need

The daughter hides anger while returning her father's untouched gift.

### Stronger `screen_to_video_brief`

```text
Source function:
Show controlled anger, not explicit confession.

Clip mode:
Single 8-second clip.

Primary action:
The daughter places the gift box back on the table too carefully.

Camera:
Slow push from medium close-up to close-up.

Lighting:
Domestic evening lamp, warm but slightly dim.

Audio:
Room tone, no score, cardboard edge brushing wood.

Invariant:
No shouting. No tears. The control is the point.

Avoid:
Do not add reconciliation energy. Do not cut away to explanatory flashbacks.
```

## 2. Weaker Bridge

```text
Angry daughter scene, emotional family drama, warm room, dramatic acting, camera movement, cinematic lighting, sadness, conflict, tension, detailed environment, nuanced psychology, symbolic gift box, powerful performance, slow motion if needed.
```

### Why The Stronger Bridge Works

- It keeps the dramatic task.
- It compresses the scene into one visual proof.
- It distinguishes core action from mood garnish.

### Why The Weaker Bridge Fails

- It keeps adding adjectives without making decisions.
- It does not say what the clip actually proves.
- It overloads the container with optionality.
