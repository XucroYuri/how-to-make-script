# Reactive Comedy Pattern Pack

This pack is a reference surface, not a comedy constitution.
Use it to compare how reaction, escalation, and backfire create repeatable comic pressure.

All fragments below are synthetic and original.

## RC-01. Small Trigger, Oversized Response

**When to use**  
When a character’s temperament is the real comic engine, and a tiny social stimulus can expose it.

**Creative problem**  
The scene wants comedy, but the joke currently lives in dialogue punchlines rather than character reaction.

**Strong fragment**
```text
INT. OFFICE PANTRY - NOON

The new intern writes her name on a mug with a marker.

QIANG freezes.

QIANG
You can do that?

INTERN
It is just a mug.

Five minutes later every mug in the pantry has a label, a rank, and an ownership chart taped to the wall.
```

**Why this works**  
The trigger is tiny. The reaction reveals a controlling personality. The escalation is emotionally logical, not random.

**Weaker contrasting fragment**
```text
INT. OFFICE PANTRY - NOON

People say random funny things about mugs.
```

**Why the weaker version fails**  
The comedy is detachable from character. Anyone could say it, and nothing escalates from it.

**Non-dogma note**  
Some comedies live on verbal wit or absurd world logic. Reaction-led comedy is strongest when personality misfires are the real attraction.

## RC-02. Too Nice, Then Backfire

**When to use**  
When the character keeps trying to do the right thing, but their inability to set boundaries drives the comic disaster.

**Creative problem**  
The script wants the audience to laugh with sympathy, not cruelty.

**Strong fragment**
```text
EXT. APARTMENT GATE - EVENING

XIAO LI agrees to sign for one neighbor's package.

Then another.
Then another.

By the time the delivery truck leaves, she is standing behind a tower of thirty-seven boxes and cannot reach her own front door.
```

**Why this works**  
The character’s niceness is not an abstract trait. It becomes a repeatable mistake pattern with visible escalation and a clean backfire image.

**Weaker contrasting fragment**
```text
EXT. APARTMENT GATE - EVENING

XIAO LI is a very kind person.
```

**Why the weaker version fails**  
It names the trait instead of staging the reaction loop that makes the trait funny.

**Non-dogma note**  
Backfire comedy works only when the audience understands why the character made the mistake. If the reaction feels arbitrary, sympathy collapses.
