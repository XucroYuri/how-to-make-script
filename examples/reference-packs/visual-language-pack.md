# Visual Language Pack Examples

These examples are teaching artifacts.
They are reference paths, not mandatory templates.

## 1. Japanese Mood-First Pack For A Quiet Family Scene

### Stronger Pack Shape

```text
Working language: Japanese + English technical anchors
Task: mood-first previz brief
Core terms:
- 間 / ma: silence and held space between lines
- 木漏れ日 / komorebi: dappled afternoon light through leaves
- バストショット / bust shot: chest-up emotional framing
- 手持ち / handheld: slight instability, not chaos
Prompt-ready phrase:
静かな食卓、間を大切に、木漏れ日、バストショット、手持ちのわずかな揺れ
Warning:
Do not confuse "ma" with empty slowness. It should sharpen relationship pressure.
```

### Weaker Pack Shape

```text
Use Japanese words to make it feel more artistic:
wabi-sabi, yugen, zen, sakura, anime, samurai
```

### Why The Stronger Pack Works

- It is task-bounded.
- The terms alter framing, light, rhythm, and silence.
- It explains the operational consequence of the cultural term.

### Why The Weaker Pack Fails

- It is decorative rather than selective.
- The words do not point to an actual scene problem.
- It turns Japanese vocabulary into branding fog.

## 2. Korean Energy Pack For A Shortform Emotional Reveal

### Stronger Pack Shape

```text
Working language: Korean with English support terms
Task: shortform emotional reveal
Core terms:
- 클로즈업: face pressure
- 돌리인: emotional intensification
- 비트싱크: edit rhythm tied to reveal beat
- 한: unresolved ache, not generic sadness
Prompt-ready phrase:
얼굴 클로즈업, 천천히 돌리인, 비트싱크 편집, 한의 정서가 남아 있는 표정
Warning:
Do not use "han" as a melodrama sticker if the scene has no underlying burden.
```

### Weaker Pack Shape

```text
Make it Korean:
K-drama, tears, rooftop, umbrella scene, sad OST
```

### Why The Stronger Pack Works

- It combines shot language, rhythm, and emotional register.
- It preserves cultural specificity without reducing it to cliche.
- It gives the downstream system something usable.

### Why The Weaker Pack Fails

- It confuses trope inventory with visual instruction.
- It tells you the reference shelf, not the execution choice.
