# Source Map: Real-World Screenplay Signals

This file maps source-backed signals to the "reality lenses" used elsewhere in the repository. It is intentionally concise. Some operational implications are direct inferences, not verbatim source claims.

## Audience / Attention

- Deloitte's 2025 Digital Media Trends report frames social platforms as a dominant force in media and entertainment, and says younger viewers are shifting toward social video, gaming, and streaming while facing price/value pressure and fragmented choice. Source: [Deloitte 2025 Digital Media Trends](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/digital-media-trends-consumption-habits-survey/2025.html).
- Nielsen reported in June 2025 that streaming reached 44.8% of total TV usage in May 2025, ahead of combined broadcast and cable at 44.2%, and said streaming usage was up 71% since May 2021. Source: [Nielsen milestone report](https://www.nielsen.com/news-center/2025/streaming-reaches-historic-tv-milestone-eclipses-combined-broadcast-and-cable-viewing-for-first-time/).
- Inference: scripts for short-form, streaming, and platform-fragmented surfaces need sharper opening pressure, clearer message singularity, and more explicit retention design than legacy linear-only assumptions.

## Industry / Commissioning

- The Motion Picture Association's 2025 CinemaCon remarks emphasize the industry's desire for great movies that draw big audiences and for innovative distribution models and viewing choices. Source: [MPA CinemaCon remarks](https://www.motionpictures.org/remarks/mpa-chairman-and-ceo-charles-rivkin-highlights-state-of-the-industry-at-2025-cinemacon/).
- The WGA's 2023 MBA summary says episodic series now have minimum staffing and minimum duration provisions for development rooms and regular writers' rooms, with details that vary by episode count and production stage. Source: [WGA MBA summary](https://www.wga.org/contracts/contracts/mba/summary-of-the-2023-wga-mba).
- The WGA staff size guide shows that post-greenlight and development rooms have concrete minimums, and that typical room sizes are often above the floor. Source: [WGA staff size guide](https://www.wga.org/members/employment-resources/writers-deal-hub/staff-size-guide).
- The WGA's AI page says AI is not a writer, AI-generated material cannot count as literary material, companies cannot require writers to use AI, and AI-generated material must be disclosed to writers. Source: [WGA AI guidance](https://www.wga.org/contracts/know-your-rights/artificial-intelligence).
- Inference: a screenplay knowledge system should treat development output as commission-aware and labor-aware, not just story-aware.

## Education / Training

- USC's Writing for Screen & Television division describes training for film, television, games, and emerging media, including short scripts, feature-length screenplays, television episodes, web series, and scripts for games and immersive media. Source: [USC Writing for Screen & Television](https://cinema.usc.edu/writing/).
- UCLA Extension offers screenwriting certificates for feature film writing, film and TV comprehensive study, and television writing. Source: [UCLA Extension screenwriting certificates](https://www.uclaextension.edu/writing/screenwriting/certificates).
- UCLA Extension's Introduction to Screenwriting course says it covers feature film, television specs and pilots, plus rotating forms such as video game writing, animation, sketch comedy writing, and script doctoring, and also looks at the business end of writing. Source: [UCLA Extension SCRIPT X 400](https://www.uclaextension.edu/writing/screenwriting/course/introduction-screenwriting-script-x-400).
- Inference: screenwriting capability is trained in stages and across formats, not learned once as a single general skill.

## History / Archive

- The Writers Guild Foundation says its Shavelson-Webb Library is devoted to the craft and history of screenwriting and holds over 45,000 produced film and television scripts. Source: [Writers Guild Foundation library](https://www.wgfoundation.org/library/).
- The Foundation's Resource Center explicitly covers craft and business of screenwriting and includes pages on Writers Guild history and historical Hollywood research. Source: [Writers Guild Foundation resource center](https://www.wgfoundation.org/resource-center).
- The Foundation's historical research guide notes that many scripts and collections live in archives and special collections, that not everything is online, and that recent scripts may not yet be available in libraries. Source: [Historical research guide](https://www.wgfoundation.org/conducting-historical-research).
- Inference: screenwriting history work requires archival literacy, not just search-engine literacy.

## Operational Use

- Use the audience / attention signals when deciding hook speed, compression, and platform fit.
- Use the industry / commissioning signals when deciding output format, staffing assumptions, and rewrite priorities.
- Use the education / training signals when designing learning paths, exercises, and skill progression.
- Use the history / archive signals when choosing analogs, testing assumptions, or explaining lineage.
