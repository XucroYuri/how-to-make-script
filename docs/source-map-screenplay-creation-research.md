# Source Map: How To Create a Screenplay

This file records public research and orientation links used to build the repository’s broad background layer for the question:

- how to create a screenplay.

It is not a ranked canon.
It is a map of useful evidence surfaces: formal training, archives, oral histories, cognitive research, development workflow, and current media conditions.

## 1. Formal training and deliberate practice

Sources:

- [USC John Wells Division of Writing for Screen & Television](https://cinema.usc.edu/writing/)
- [UCLA TFT Professional Program in Screenwriting](https://professionalprograms.tft.ucla.edu/screenwriting/)
- [UCLA Extension Screenwriting](https://www.uclaextension.edu/writing/screenwriting)
- [AFI Conservatory Screenwriting](https://conservatory.afi.com/screenwriting/)
- [NYU Tisch Dramatic Writing Graduate Program](https://tisch.nyu.edu/dramatic-writing/graduateprogram)
- [Sundance Institute Feature Film Program](https://www.sundance.org/programs/feature-film/)
- [Academy Nicholl Fellowships FAQ PDF](https://www.oscars.org/sites/oscars/files/2023_nicholl_faq_1_0.pdf)

Observed signals:

- screenwriting is trained across multiple containers, not just feature-film pages;
- serious programs attach writing to workshops, revision, mentorship, and full-length sustainability;
- cross-medium exposure is treated as skill-building rather than distraction;
- screenplay judgment is subjective, but institutions still use repeated reading, submission, revision, and selection cycles to surface durable signals.

Repo inference:

- broad “how to create a screenplay” answers should foreground iterative artifact practice and rewrite loops, not only theory summaries.

## 2. Archive literacy and document-chain reality

Sources:

- [Oscars / Margaret Herrick Library Scripts Collection](https://www.oscars.org/margaret-herrick-library/collections/scripts)
- [Writers Guild Foundation Archive](https://www.wgfoundation.org/archive)
- [Writers Guild Foundation: The Writer Speaks](https://www.wgfoundation.org/the-writer-speaks)

- [Television Academy Foundation oral history example: David E. Kelley](https://www.televisionacademy.com/features/emmy-magazine/articles/foundation-interview-david-e-kelley)

Observed signals:

- “the screenplay” exists as a document family, not just a final draft;
- annotated drafts, oral histories, production materials, and unproduced work are serious evidence for process knowledge;
- provenance, date, variant status, and version lineage matter.

Repo inference:

- the repo should preserve document-chain awareness, not flatten all learning to polished final pages.

## 3. Audience cognition and narrative processing

Sources:

- [Zacks et al. 2009: Segmentation in Reading and Film Comprehension](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19397386/)
- [Kosie & Baldwin 2019: Attentional profiles linked to event segmentation are robust to missing information](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s41235-019-0157-4)

- [Song et al. 2021: Neural signatures of attentional engagement during narratives and its consequences for event memory](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34385312/)
- [Ki et al. 2016: Attention Strongly Modulates Reliability of Neural Responses to Naturalistic Narrative Stimuli](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6601758/)
- [Green & Brock 2000: The role of transportation in the persuasiveness of public narratives](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11079236/)
- [Yeshurun et al. 2017: Same Story, Different Story](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28099068/)
- [Masís-Obando et al. 2022: Schema representations in distinct brain networks support narrative memory](https://elifesciences.org/articles/70445)
- [Chiera et al. 2022: Does the character-based dimension of stories impact narrative processing?](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35048215/)
- [Gernsbacher et al. on emotional-state representation](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25309018/)
- [Cutting 2016: Cognitive Research on film style, attention, pace and narrative](https://cognitiveresearchjournal.springeropen.com/counter/pdf/10.1186/s41235-016-0029-0.pdf)

Observed signals:

- viewers actively segment, infer, frame, remember, and emotionally model stories;
- scene boundaries and causal integration points matter disproportionately;
- emotional clarity and coherence are processing variables, not just decorative craft concerns;
- too much surface explanation can damage transportation and absorption.

Repo inference:

- screenplay craft should treat comprehension, attention, and memory as design surfaces.

- support roles, attribution, version lineage, and continuity are part of screenplay reality, not admin afterthoughts.

Repo inference:

- the agent should support room-aware and development-aware screenplay creation, not only solo page drafting.

## 5. Current media conditions and industrial context

Sources:

- [Deloitte 2025 Digital Media Trends](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/technology/digital-media-trends-consumption-habits-survey/2025.html)
- [Nielsen June 2025 streaming milestone](https://www.nielsen.com/news-center/2025/streaming-reaches-historic-tv-milestone-eclipses-combined-broadcast-and-cable-viewing-for-first-time/)
- [Motion Picture Association 2025 CinemaCon remarks](https://www.motionpictures.org/remarks/mpa-chairman-and-ceo-charles-rivkin-highlights-state-of-the-industry-at-2025-cinemacon/)

Observed signals:

- premium storytelling now competes with social video and streaming fragmentation for attention and economics;
- streaming is no longer a side condition, but a primary viewing reality;
- “great stories, well told” remains the constant, but the containers and attention markets around that constant have changed.

Repo inference:

- screenplay creation should remain story-first while still modeling platform and commissioning pressure where it materially changes the next creative decision.

## Adoption rule

The repository should absorb:

- durable research-backed abstractions;
- public source maps for future contributors;
- lens-specific inferences that can be loaded without loading the whole repo.

The repository should not absorb:

- private notes as hidden authority;
- inaccessible guru claims without public grounding;
- one method family rebranded as universal research truth.
