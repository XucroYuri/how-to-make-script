# Source Map: Screenplay Method Families

This file records public orientation links for screenplay-method families that informed the repository's open abstractions.
These links are not treated as one-true authorities. They are public entry points for future contributors who need a shared baseline.

## 1. Loglines, beats, and structure surfaces

Sources:
- [Final Draft: Logline, Story Beats and Brainstorming](https://www.finaldraft.com/learn/loglines-story-beats-brainstorming/)
- [Final Draft: What Is a Story Beat?](https://www.finaldraft.com/blog/what-is-a-story-beat)
- [Final Draft: Building Screenplay Structure](https://www.finaldraft.com/learn/building-screenplay-structure/)
- [Save the Cat essential vocabulary PDF](https://savethecat.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/STC_Crash_Course_Essential_Vocabulary.pdf)
- [Save the Cat at-a-glance movie sheet PDF](https://savethecat.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/STC-At-The-Movies-At-A-Glance.pdf)
- [StudioBinder: Save the Cat Beat Sheet Explained](https://www.studiobinder.com/blog/save-the-cat-beat-sheet/)
- [StudioBinder: What is a Story Beat in a Screenplay?](https://www.studiobinder.com/blog/story-beat-in-screenplay/)

Observed signals:
- logline and beat work are widely treated as development surfaces, not just formatting exercises;
- beat language is useful, but no single beat family honestly covers every project;
- software-facing beat boards and beat sheets are most useful when they surface the right unit of change.

Repo inference:
- the repo should separate `structure family selection` from `beat carrier selection`;
- loglines need false-positive protection so polished premise sentences do not masquerade as actual story engines.

## 2. Opening design and attention entry

Sources:
- [StudioBinder: Screenplay Opening Scene Examples](https://www.studiobinder.com/blog/screenplay-opening-scene-example/)
- [StudioBinder: In Medias Res](https://www.studiobinder.com/blog/in-medias-res-definition-examples/)

Observed signals:
- effective openings do different jobs: immediate conflict, premise promise, question gap, or character/world orientation;
- opening energy and opening clarity are related but not identical.

Repo inference:
- the repo should treat opening design as `job selection`, not as a checklist of tricks;
- a strong opening must promise a real viewing contract, not just win one second of attention.

## 3. Mythic and non-mythic structure families

Sources:
- [Joseph Campbell Foundation: The Collected Works / The Hero with a Thousand Faces](https://www.jcf.org/learn/joseph-campbell-collected-works)
- [Joseph Campbell Foundation: Myth, Campbell & Film](https://www.jcf.org/post/myth-campbell-film)

Observed signals:
- hero's-journey language remains influential, especially for quest and transformation stories;
- mythic structure is powerful, but not universal, and should not erase other structure families.

Repo inference:
- heroic structure belongs in the repo as one structure family among several, not as a default law;
- structure selection should stay engine-first, not prestige-first.

## 4. Screenplay to visual execution boundaries

Sources:
- [StudioBinder: Screenplay Formatting / Path to Production](https://www.studiobinder.com/screenplay-formatting/)
- [StudioBinder: Storyboard template PDF](https://www.studiobinder.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/StudioBinder-Storyboard-Template-2-Frames-Landscape.pdf)
- [StudioBinder Support: Scene heading best practices](https://support.studiobinder.com/en/articles/3032128-best-practices-when-formatting-a-scene-heading-in-a-screenplay)

Observed signals:
- screenplay, shot planning, storyboards, and production-facing packets are adjacent but distinct surfaces;
- pre-production tooling becomes more reliable when screenplay structure remains legible and traceable.

Repo inference:
- bridge artifacts should preserve source traceability, visible asset grounding, and honest container boundaries;
- storyboard- or clip-facing detail should not collapse screenplay truth into vendor syntax or render-only noise.

## Adoption Rule

The repository should absorb:

- durable abstractions;
- public baseline references;
- source maps that help contributors orient themselves.

It should not absorb:

- private local-note paths;
- inaccessible workshop materials as hidden authority;
- product-bound prompt shells as universal screenplay doctrine.
